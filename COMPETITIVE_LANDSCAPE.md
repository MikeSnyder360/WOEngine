# WOEngine: Competitive Landscape

## Overview

WOEngine targets fitness coaches/entrepreneurs who want to:
- Create branded fitness apps (iOS + Android)
- Customize workout programs without coding
- Distribute via their own App Store/Play accounts
- Pay a subscription (customization) + per-build fee (distribution)

**Market reality**: This is NOT a unique gap. There are 17+ white-label fitness app builder platforms competing in this space, ranging from $25–600+/month.

**WOEngine's positioning**: Mid-to-premium tier with emphasis on **customization depth** + **hybrid pricing** + **zero commission**.

## COMPLETE COMPETITOR LANDSCAPE (17+ Platforms)

### **TIER 1: Enterprise/Mid-Market White-Label Platforms**

#### **Trainerize (ABC Trainerize)**
- **Pricing**: $9–23/month base; $248/month for Studio Plus (white-label ready); white-label app is add-on
- **Target**: Personal trainers, small studios
- **Key**: Custom branded app, nutrition coaching, video coaching, Stripe payments
- **Launch time**: Varies by App Store approval
- **App ownership**: Customers own published app
- **Revenue model**: Monthly SaaS + white-label add-on
- **vs. WOEngine**: Similar add-on model; Trainerize is bundled all-in-one; WOEngine likely offers more customization

#### **Exercise.com**
- **Pricing**: Custom/enterprise (no public pricing; requires sales call)
- **Target**: Gyms, fitness influencers, large businesses
- **Key**: Fully custom app + website, 5% transaction fee on e-commerce
- **Launch time**: ~6 weeks onboarding
- **App ownership**: Customers own branded apps
- **Revenue model**: Enterprise SaaS + 5% transaction fee
- **vs. WOEngine**: Higher-end; less price-sensitive market; more hands-on implementation

#### **My PT Hub**
- **Pricing**: EUR 25–59/month (base); EUR 145/month (white-label); EUR 95 one-time custom branding
- **Target**: Personal trainers, small coaching businesses
- **Key**: Unlimited workout/nutrition plans, white-label iOS/Android, 4–6 week launch
- **App ownership**: Customers own published app
- **Revenue model**: Tiered monthly + white-label add-on
- **vs. WOEngine**: More affordable; simpler alternative; customers don't own code but own distribution

### **TIER 2: No-Code/Low-Code Builders (Creator/Coach Focus)**

#### **Passion.io**
- **Pricing**: $99–119/month (Launch/Scale); $10k–20k for done-for-you service; 3.9% + Stripe 2.9% + $0.30 transaction fees
- **Target**: Fitness creators, online coaches, influencers
- **Key**: Drag-and-drop, community, drip content, IAP, 30-day money-back guarantee
- **Launch time**: 30 days (with PassionPlus service)
- **App ownership**: Customers own branded apps
- **Revenue model**: Monthly SaaS + transaction fees (15–30% on IAP)
- **vs. WOEngine**: Creator-focused; transaction fees add friction; faster with done-for-you option

#### **Movement.so** ⭐ (Most Similar)
- **Pricing**: Freemium model; free tier available; paid tiers pricing not fully disclosed
- **Target**: Fitness coaches, personal trainers, creators
- **Key**: Drag-and-drop, templates, community, white-label publishing, $0 platform fee on monetization (97.5% to creator)
- **Launch time**: Not specified
- **App ownership**: Customers own published app
- **Revenue model**: Freemium + paid subscription
- **vs. WOEngine**: Nearly identical positioning; free entry point vs. WOEngine's likely paid model; emphasizes ease of use

#### **BuildFire**

- **Target**: Small gyms, trainers, fitness startups
- **Key**: Fitness templates, drag-and-drop, no coding, class schedules, memberships
- **Launch time**: Not specified
- **App ownership**: Customers own published app
- **Revenue model**: Monthly SaaS
- **vs. WOEngine**: Lower-cost entry; less sophisticated; generic (not fitness-specialized)

#### **Appy Pie**

### **TIER 3: Premium White-Label Specialists**

#### **Ymove** ⭐⭐ (Strongest Direct Competitor)
- **Pricing**: $39+/month; 14-day free trial; $0 commission on customer payments
- **Target**: Fitness coaches, trainers, yoga studios, creators
- **Key**: White-label iOS/Android in app stores, video hosting, workouts, nutrition, in-app purchases, 100% revenue keep
- **Launch time**: 2 weeks
- **App ownership**: Customers own branded apps (under their own developer accounts)
- **Revenue model**: Monthly SaaS; zero commission
- **vs. WOEngine**: Nearly identical value proposition; lower starting price ($39 vs. likely higher); same zero-commission philosophy

- **App ownership**: Customers own apps (under their own developer accounts)
- **Revenue model**: Monthly SaaS; zero commission structure
- **vs. WOEngine**: Competitive pricing ($149); zero commission; faster (4 weeks); strong value prop

#### **Kahunas**

- **Target**: Personal trainers, fitness coaches
- **Key**: Branded app (Ultimate), client profiles, nutrition/workout tracking, Stripe/PayPal, zero commission
- **Launch time**: Not specified
- **App ownership**: Customers own published app
- **Revenue model**: Tiered monthly SaaS; zero commission
- **vs. WOEngine**: Competitive pricing; white-label gated to highest tier (less accessible); strong feature set

#### **Virtuagym**
- **Pricing**: €29–489/month (quote-based); white-label adds significant cost
- **Target**: Gyms, fitness studios, trainers
- **Key**: Workout templates, booking, white-label app, modular pricing

- **Revenue model**: Monthly SaaS with modular add-ons

#### **Everfit**

#### **Lean On Digital** (Premium, Done-for-You)
- **Pricing**: Custom/enterprise (not publicly disclosed)
- **Target**: Premium fitness professionals
- **Key**: Fully custom white-label iOS/Android, customer's developer accounts, done-for-you service, 7–10 day turnaround
- **App ownership**: Customers own developer accounts
- **Revenue model**: Subscription-based (exact pricing unknown)

#### **FitSW**

### SmartGym

**What it is**: iOS app for end-users to create and customize workouts

**Market position**:
- Consumer fitness app (like Nike Training Club, BeachBody, Peloton)
- Users download → customize workouts within the app
- **Not a builder platform** (coaches don't create branded apps)

**Why it's not a direct competitor**:
- SmartGym is the **app itself** (end-user product)
- WOEngine is a **platform to build branded apps** (coach/entrepreneur product)
- Different markets: B2C (SmartGym) vs. B2B (WOEngine)

**Relevance to WOEngine**:
- SmartGym proves the market wants customizable workouts
- Shows UI/UX patterns for workout customization (could inform WOEngine's in-app program editor)
- Not a threat to WOEngine's positioning (coaches want to own their apps, not use someone else's branded app)

## Market Gaps

### What WOEngine Solves

1. **Customization + Security**: Unlike Flutterflow/Adalo (code visible), unlike Zen Planner (UI fixed), WOEngine offers both
2. **Fitness-First**: Unlike BuildFire (generic), WOEngine ships with a complete HIIT app + customizable program data
3. **Customer Distribution Ownership**: Unlike most (which publish to App Store on your behalf), WOEngine lets customers keep their own accounts
4. **Hybrid Pricing**: Subscription (customization) + per-build fee (distribution) is uncommon; most do subscription-only
5. **Scalable Multi-Tenancy**: One EAS project serving many customers; most white-label platforms don't address this

### Remaining Questions

- **Market maturity**: Are fitness coaches willing to pay subscription + per-build, or do they expect all-in-one pricing?
- **Feature scope**: How much program customization do coaches actually want vs. template presets?

## Key Market Insights

### **Price Positioning**
- **Entry-level**: $25–40/month (Everfit free, My PT Hub EUR 25, Movement.so freemium)
- **Mid-market**: $70–150/month (Ymove $39+, FitBudd $149, Kahunas $99)
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing (Exercise.com, Virtuagym, Lean On Digital)

**WOEngine opportunity**: If positioning at $100–200/month, directly competes with FitBudd/Kahunas tier. If per-build fee is $20–50, hybrid model could be more attractive to power users.

### **Market Trends**
1. **Zero commission is table stakes** — Most leaders (Ymove, FitBudd, Kahunas, My PT Hub) offer 100% revenue to creators
2. **Freemium gaining traction** — Movement.so, Everfit free entry → conversion funnel
3. **No-code is becoming standard** — Passion.io, Movement.so, Appy Pie leading ease-of-use
4. **Launch speed matters** — FitSW (1–2 weeks), Ymove (2 weeks), FitBudd (4 weeks) advertise aggressively
5. **White-label is standard, not premium** — Even $39/month plans include it (Ymove)

### **WOEngine's Potential Advantages**
✅ **Customization depth** — Pre-built HIIT engine likely exceeds drag-and-drop builders  
✅ **Closed-source** — If advertised as IP protection, differentiates from open/exportable code platforms  
✅ **Hybrid pricing** — Per-build fee is unusual; could appeal to high-volume coaches  
✅ **Zero commission** — Must match market leaders (Ymove, FitBudd, Kahunas)  
✅ **Runtime program fetch** — Allows unlimited program updates without rebuilds (cost advantage vs. per-build competitors)

### **WOEngine's Potential Weaknesses**
❌ **Crowded market** — 17+ competitors means differentiation is critical  
❌ **Price unknown** — If >$150/month, loses to FitBudd ($149); if >$200, much harder to justify  
❌ **Launch speed not clear** — Competitors advertise 2–4 weeks; WOEngine's orchestrator + EAS may be slower  
❌ **Feature completeness** — Competitors include nutrition, billing, CRM, scheduling; WOEngine is workout-only (currently)  
❌ **No freemium** — Movement.so, Everfit, Passion.io free tiers lower barrier to entry  
❌ **Market education** — "Custom workout program in JSON" appeals to technical coaches, not mainstream

## Recommendations for WOEngine

### **1. Clarify Pricing & Positioning**
- What's the monthly subscription cost? (Suggested: $99–149 to compete with FitBudd/Kahunas)
- What's the per-build fee? (Suggested: $20–50 to align with $29 estimate)
- Is there a free tier or free trial? (Movement.so freemium, Passion.io free trial)

### **2. Emphasize Differentiation**
- **If customization is key**: Highlight pre-built HIIT engine vs. blank-slate builders (vs. Passion.io, Movement.so)
- **If IP protection is key**: Emphasize Hermes bytecode + closed-source vs. exportable code (vs. Flutterflow, Adalo)
- **If developer experience is key**: Highlight zero commission + customer ownership (vs. Fittr's marketplace model)
- **If speed is key**: Ensure EAS builds are competitive with 2–4 week launches (vs. FitSW, Ymove, FitBudd)

### **3. Consider Feature Expansion**
- Current: Workouts + branding + builds
- Competitors include: Nutrition plans, client messaging, progress tracking, wearable integration, CRM, booking
- **Priority**: Nutrition plans (most common add-on) and progress tracking (retention driver)

### **4. Plan Go-to-Market**
- **Free tier or trial** to lower entry friction (vs. freemium leaders: Movement.so, Everfit)
- **Target segment**: Coaches already running HIIT or fitness programs (natural fit for pre-built engine)
- **Early adopters**: Offer white-label done-for-you service (like Passion.io, Lean On Digital) at premium tier

### **5. Validate Customer Fit**
Before launch, validate with target coaches:
- Is $99–199/month + per-build pricing compelling vs. $39/month flat (Ymove)?
- Do they value customization depth, or prefer simplicity?
- Would they pay more for "done-for-you" onboarding?
- Is HIIT/fitness-specific engine valuable, or do they want blank slate?
